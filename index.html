I started as a Mechanical Engineer, cutting my teeth in project and operations management at Shell. In 2020, the Commonwealth Scholarship allowed me to pursue a Master's in Engineering Business Management at the University of Warwick. I left with a sharper curiosity about technology and a dissertation on blockchain in supply chain management that has since been cited 36 times.

After Warwick I joined Airlift, a hypergrowth startup where I wrote my first SQL queries; a skill that became my bread and butter. From there I moved to Zindigi, a fintech backed by one of Pakistan's largest banks, as a Product Manager. I owned three product verticals end-to-end: from ideation and development to their launch and growth. Fast-moving, high-stakes, and exactly the kind of work that built the wide horizontal bar of my T.

At JPG Store, a small team building on the Cardano blockchain, I inherited a nearly non-existent analytics setup and turned it into a critical function: organising a chaotic query library, building a unified dashboard, implementing event tracking and heatmaps, and replacing a $30k/year vendor with an internal tool. For the first time, the business could make product decisions based on data instead of experience alone.

Today I sit at the intersection of data and product: translating ambiguous business questions into clean models, and turning those models into decisions people actually trust.
